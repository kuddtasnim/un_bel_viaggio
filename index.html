    Munich Transit

    Prague → Munich → midnight bus
    Situation

    Worth spending 3h in Munich before the midnight bus, or arrive late? Travelling without luggage.

    Solution — yes, go to the city
    • ZOB & Hbf are 5 min walk apart (Arnulfstraße 21)
    • U-Bahn from Hbf to Marienplatz: 10 min
    • Hofbräuhaus open until midnight, 15 min from ZOB
    • Leave at 23:15 to be safe
    💡 Insight for next time

    Without luggage, never wait at a station. A 2–3h window at a transit city is a bonus, not a loss.

    🇩🇪 Germanytransitno luggagenight

    Beijing Airport → Centre

    PEK → hotel in city centre
    Situation

    First time in Beijing — taxi or public transport?

    Solution
    • Airport Express to Dongzhimen (~30 min, ~25 CNY)
    • Transfer to metro — reach anywhere in centre
    • Taxi in rush hour can take 1.5–2h
    💡 Insight for next time

    In Beijing, airport express is always the first choice. Download offline metro map before landing.

    🇨🇳 Chinaairportmetrologistics

    Waterfalls in Sochi

    Sochi — nature route
    Situation

    How to reach the waterfalls — almost no info, bus schedules unclear.

    Solution
    • Taxi to trailhead saves ~2h vs waiting for a bus
    • Cost reasonable when split
    • Bus exists but rare and unreliable schedule
    💡 Insight for next time

    For nature routes in Russia off-season: always budget for a taxi. Bus only if schedule confirmed in advance.

    🇷🇺 Russianaturetaxilogistics
    🏔️

    Great Wall exit at Badaling

    Badaling section → Express train
    Situation

    How to get back from the wall exit to the express train — not signposted clearly.

    Solution
    • Exit via the south gate, not the north
    • Shuttle buses run to the train station
    • Check the route on the spot — maps don't always match reality
    💡 Insight for next time

    Always locate the return route on arrival, before exploring. 10 min spent on this at the start saves 1h of confusion later.

    🇨🇳 ChinatransitGreat Wall

    About

    Un Bel Viaggio

    A personal tool for conscious travel. Not a booking platform — a place to plan intentionally, reflect deeply, and remember what actually mattered.

    Four core moments

    • Before — AI trip planning around your rhythm and interests
    • During & after — reflection, notes, scans, multilingual publishing
    • Between trips — inspiration, traveller profile, knowledge base
    • Packing — smart lists built on your past experience

    Inspired by Polarsteps — features worth borrowing

    Steps concept

    Each day of travel = a "step" with location, photos, notes. Creates a visual journey timeline automatically.

    Route on a real map

    Pins showing your actual path across a world map. Beautifully visual, tells the story of the route.

    Auto-generated stats

    Total distance travelled, days away, countries visited — calculated automatically from your steps.

    Printed travel book

    Export your trip as a beautifully formatted PDF book — text, photos, map included. Worth implementing.

    Weather per step

    Automatic weather data for each day of travel, attached to the journal entry. Useful for reflection.

    Offline access

    Full functionality without internet — essential when abroad. Critical for a travel app.

    Tech stack

    • React + Vite + TypeScript + Tailwind CSS
    • Supabase — database (PostgreSQL), Auth, file Storage
    • OpenAI API — reflection summaries, translations (RU/EN/FR)
    • Mapbox — interactive trip map
    • Web Speech API — voice recording & transcription
